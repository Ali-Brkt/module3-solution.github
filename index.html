Chicken

She heard the soft clucking behind her. That didn't bode well. What could be troubling her chickens this late at night, in this quiet part of the farm? And at this particular moment, just after she'd managed to save enough money to fix the old, creaking coop. Was there a cunning fox who'd had the same idea, and was now watching her flock, waiting for a chance to snatch the fruit of her labor? Or did the clucking mean that one of her chickens was sick and was just waiting to spread the illness to the others? She hurriedly looked all around. Suddenly she saw the shadow by the barn. Like lightning, she darted off to the left and disappeared between the two large haystacks, almost falling over the rake lying in the middle of the path. She tried to calmly assess the situation in the dim moonlight and suddenly stiffened: there was no sign of a predator; she would have to look elsewhere. The clucking got louder and louder; she saw the white plumage of a hen fluttering in the night. Is this the first sign of trouble? she thought, pressing herself back against the barn wall, trying to make her presence unknown, was all that planning and effort wasted? She was cold now, her palms damp with dew; she could feel her heart racing. Suddenly next to her, with a barely noticeable creak, a window swung quietly to and fro in the night's breeze. Could this be the vantage point she needed? Slowly she slid toward the window, pressing herself more and more into the wall, into the dark, away from her precious flock. Would this window reveal the source of her anxiety? Looking through, she saw themâ€”all her chickens, safe and sound, nestled together in their coop. It was then that she spotted it, a small, newly hatched chick, separated from the brood, chirping loudly for its mother. A wave of relief washed over her; the clucking was not a signal of danger, but the natural, soothing calls between a mother hen and her lost chick. Smiling, she walked toward the coop, ready to reunite the small family and finally put her worries to rest. Tonight, under her vigilant care, the chickens would sleep peacefully, and she could go back to her warm bed, heart full and content with the small, lively farm she nurtured every day.
